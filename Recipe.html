Lets create together!

Homemade gnocchi. Sound intimidating? Just take a deep breath because this recipe requires only a few ingredients and can be done with no fancy equipment! By the end of the day you will have all the skills and confidence of Michelin star chef Gordon Ramsay!

While making gnocchi from scratch sounds tricky, the actual process is pretty easy and well worth the effort. This recipe for homemade gnocchi is a family tradition, though making gnocchi can be done in several different ways.

Once you know how to make gnocchi, what sauces you choose to pair it with is up to you and your palate!

Note - no fancy tools are necessary to make this, however some specialized tools will certainly help! I have included a tools resource below. Also, to choose the right type of potato. You need a potato that is starchy with minimum water content like a Russet potato.

Now that you have your potatoes, you are ready to get started! Follow along below;

Ingredients

  • 4 Potatoes
  • 1 Egg
  • 2 Cups Flour
  • Olive Oil
  • Salt

    • *General rule of thumb* 1 medium-sized potato per serving or person. For every potato, use approximately 1/2 cup of flour

Tools

  • Potato Ricer
  • Peeler
  • Sieve
  • Measuring Cups
  • Bench Scraper
  • Baking Sheet
  • Large Pot

Homemade Gnocchi Instructions

  1. The first step is to prepare the workspace by cleaning the countertop. Once the workspace is clean begin by peeling the potatoes and discarding the peels in a compost or trash bin.

  2. Next step, fill a bowl with cold water to place peeled potatoes in. Let the potatoes soak. (this will help to remove excess starch) Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil and add the potatoes. Cover with a lid and let boil for 20 - 25 min.

    Use a fork to check on the potatoes. "Fork Tender" when the fork presses into potatoes without much force, it is fork tender.

    Once tender, take potatoes out of water and allow them to cool in a colander to get rid of any excess water.

  3. On the countertop, scoop two cups of flour into the sieve and sift onto clean counter space. Then, using the measuring cup make a well in the pile of flour. Form the well large enough to fit the other ingredients.

  4. Next, crack an egg into a cup and set it aside. Using the potato ricer, rice the potatoes into the well. Then, pour the cracked egg into the well, and using a fork, whisk the egg briefly.

    Then using your bench scraper, (or using your hands) gently combine all the ingredients together and knead to form the dough. knead dough, sifting flour as needed,
    *The SECRET to perfect gnocchi lies in this step! Be mindful of overmixing and overflouring it! Knead just enough for the dough to come together. It should have a loose, airy texture (not gooey nor dense.) The best time to add flour is at the beginning and end. The biggest culprit to chewy homemade gnocchi is too much flour.

  5. Once the dough ball is complete, lightly Sprinkle it with salt.

    Then put the dough in bowl and cover. Let it sit for roughly 30 minutes.

  6. Dust the countertop with flour and put the dough on floured surface.

    Then using a bench scraper cut the ball into 4 pieces.

    Take one piece, cover the remaining 3, and roll it into a log about 1" thick.

    Repeat process with the remaining dough pieces.

  7. Dust the baking sheets with corn meal and toss cut pieces in corn meal to prevent sticking.

    Using a gnocchi board (a fork will do) roll gnocchi on board or fork to create grooves. The indentations on your gnocchi will help the sauce stick to them better.

    Repeat until all the gnocchi is rolled.

  8. Before you cook, gently shake away any excess flour from your gnocchi.

    Add the gnocchi to a pot of boiling water and cook until the gnocchi float to the top

    When they rise to the top, remove with a slotted spoon and drain well, about 2 to 4 minutes.

  • Heat up your favorite sauce to pair with the fresh gnocchi! A few personal favorites; a pomodoro, a fresh basil & pesto, or even a creamy butter and sage sauce!

    Buon Appetito!

  • Additional Notes - Gnocchi freeze very well.

    Uncooked gnocchi can be frozen on the baking sheet, then transferred to a zip-top bag and frozen for up to 2 months.

    To cook them, just drop the frozen gnocchi directly in the boiling water without defrosting.
